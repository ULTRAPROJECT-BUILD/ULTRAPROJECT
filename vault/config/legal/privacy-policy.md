## 6. De-Identification of Data

### 6.1 What De-Identification Means

De-identification is the process of removing or transforming personal and business identifiers so that data can no longer reasonably be linked to a specific individual or organization. This includes removal of:

- Names, email addresses, phone numbers, and physical addresses.
- Business names, brand names, and proprietary terminology.
- Any unique identifiers or contextual details that could enable re-identification.

### 6.2 How We Use De-Identified Data

- Develop new service features.
- Generate aggregate insights about service usage patterns.
- Conduct internal research and development.
